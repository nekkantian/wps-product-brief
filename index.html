Overview

The WPS Execution Platform is an extension to Microsoft Dynamics 365 Finance and Operations (D365 F&O) for managing shop-floor execution and field execution. It complements D365 F&O by providing operational workflows, mobile data capture, and traceability across production, quality, dispatch readiness, installation, commissioning, and after-sales service activities, while keeping D365 F&O as the system of record.

Scope

  • Shop-floor execution for production orders, routes, and operations.
  • Quality inspections and approvals across manufacturing and field stages.
  • Dispatch readiness checks and evidence capture.
  • Field execution for installation and commissioning, including digital customer sign-off.
  • Issue and exception management with escalation workflows.
  • Planner and service visibility for factory and site jobs (SLA and milestone tracking).

Out of scope / boundaries

  • Does not replace D365 F&O planning, costing, or financial posting logic; these remain in D365 F&O.
  • Relies on masters and core transactions maintained in D365 F&O (e.g., items, production orders, projects, assets).

Operational gaps addressed

In many manufacturing environments using ERP systems, execution data is captured across spreadsheets, paper checklists, and isolated tools. Common gaps include limited shop-floor visibility, manual or fragmented quality tracking, offline commissioning records, and scattered service history. These gaps typically lead to delays, rework, and increased audit and compliance effort.

Platform at a glance

The platform provides a unified execution layer aligned to D365 F&O entities. It supports controlled status transitions, role-based dashboards, standardized inspection templates, and mobile technician workflows. Execution events and evidence are captured with a consistent audit trail and are associated to production orders, serial numbers, and assets as applicable.

D365 F&O remains the authoritative source for masters and financial records. The execution platform captures operational events, inspection outcomes, and evidence references and associates them back to relevant D365 F&O records via secure APIs.

Capability summary (text-only)

This section retains a text-only summary of capabilities for contexts where screenshots are not shared (e.g., procurement, security review).

Quality traceability and approvals

  • Linkage of quality records to production order, serial number, and asset identifiers as applicable.
  • Approval workflows for deviations and quality releases.
  • Audit-ready logs with user, timestamp, and reason-code history.

Technician and field execution (mobile)

  • Android application for technicians and inspectors.
  • Guided checklists aligned to operations and inspection plans.
  • Start / stop / hold controls for task execution with reason capture.
  • On-device media capture for documentation and evidence.

Planner and service visibility

  • Unified view of factory jobs and site jobs for planners and service coordinators.
  • SLA and milestone tracking for commissioning and service activities.

Integration with D365 F&O

The platform exchanges data with D365 F&O using secure APIs. D365 F&O remains the system of record for masters and finance. The execution platform captures operational events, inspection outcomes, and evidence references and associates them back to relevant D365 F&O records.

Primary touchpoints

  • Production orders and related routes/operations.
  • Sales orders and/or project records driving manufacturing and field work.
  • Assets and warranty references for installed equipment.
  • Finance-ready execution outcomes to support downstream posting in D365 F&O (based on customer configuration).

Security and governance

  • Role-based access control aligned to organizational roles (planner, supervisor, QA, technician, service coordinator, administrator).
  • End-to-end audit trails for status changes, approvals, and evidence capture.
  • Secure storage for media evidence with controlled access and retention policies (deployment-dependent).
  • Reason codes and mandatory remarks for holds, reassignments, and deviations to improve accountability.

Expected operational outcomes

Typical outcomes from implementing structured execution and traceability include improved schedule adherence, reduced rework from early detection of quality issues, faster closure of commissioning activities, and simpler preparation for internal and external audits. Actual results depend on baseline process maturity, adoption, and configuration.

Target deployments

Ideal customer profile

  • Manufacturers running D365 F&O who require controlled execution tracking beyond standard ERP transactions.
  • Make-to-Order (MTO) and Engineer-to-Order (ETO) environments with complex routing, inspections, and site work.
  • Capital equipment and project-driven industries where factory and field execution must be traceable to serial and asset records.

Typical implementation considerations

  • Define execution stages, status transitions, reason codes, and approval workflows required by the organization.
  • Configure inspection templates and parameter sets for manufacturing and commissioning steps.
  • Map D365 F&O entities and identifiers to ensure consistent linkage (production order, serial, project, asset).
  • Set up user roles and access controls for factory and field users.
  • Establish policies for media evidence storage, retention, and access.
